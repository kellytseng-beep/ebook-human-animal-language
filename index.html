Human language is a unique system of communication溝通 · noun that allows people to share complex ideas, emotions, and experiences. Unlike animals, humans can use language to talk about the past, present, and future. This ability is called displacement位移性 · noun. For example, a person can describe what happened yesterday or plan for tomorrow. Animals, however, usually communicate about immediate即時的 · adjective needs, such as food or danger.

Another important feature of human language is creativity創造力 · noun. Humans can produce an unlimited number of sentences using a limited set of words and rules. This means we can express表達 · verb new ideas that have never been said before. In contrast, animal communication systems are more fixed固定的 · adjective. For instance, bees perform a dance to show the location of food, but this system does not change much or allow new meanings.

Human language also has a complex structure結構 · noun. It includes grammar, which is a set of rules that organizes words into meaningful sentences. These rules help us understand each other clearly. Animal communication does not have the same level of structure. While some animals, like dolphins or birds, can produce different sounds, these sounds do not follow detailed grammatical文法的 · adjective rules like human language.

Finally, human language is learned socially and culturally. Children learn language by interacting互動 · verb with others in their community. Culture plays a big role in shaping how language is used. On the other hand, many animal communication systems are mostly instinctive本能的 · adjective. This means animals are born with the ability to communicate in certain ways, without needing to learn complex rules from others.

Section 3
Grammar Patterns
1. "allows someone to + verb"
Used to show permission or ability.
Language allows people to share ideas.
This app allows users to chat online. · 這個應用程式讓使用者可以聊天。
The teacher allows students to ask questions. · 老師允許學生發問。
2. "Unlike + noun"
Used to show contrast.
Unlike animals, humans use complex language.
Unlike him, she is very patient. · 不像他，她很有耐心。
Unlike summer, winter is cold. · 不像夏天，冬天很冷。
3. "This means (that)..."
Used to explain or clarify.
This means we can express new ideas.
He is late. This means he missed the bus. · 他遲到了，這表示他錯過公車。
She studies hard. This means she will succeed. · 她努力學習，這表示她會成功。
4. "While + clause"
Used to show contrast.
While animals communicate, they lack grammar.
While I like coffee, I prefer tea. · 雖然我喜歡咖啡，但我更喜歡茶。
While he is young, he is very wise. · 雖然他年輕，但他很聰明。
5. "without + V-ing"
Used to show absence of an action.
...without needing to learn rules.
He left without saying goodbye. · 他沒有說再見就離開了。
She passed the test without studying. · 她沒有讀書就通過考試。

Answers & Explanations

Part A — Multiple Choice Answers

1.B  — Displacement means talking about past and future, not just the present moment.
2.C  — Humans can create an unlimited number of sentences from a limited set of words.
3.C  — Animals usually communicate about immediate needs such as food or danger.
4.B  — Grammar is a set of rules that organizes words into meaningful sentences.
5.C  — Children learn language through social interaction with their community.

Part B — Cloze Answers

1.immediate  — Animals react to dangers or needs happening right now.
2.structure  — Human language follows grammatical rules that give it a complex structure.
3.communication  — Language is the primary tool of human communication.
4.instinctive  — Many animal communication patterns are innate, not learned from others.
5.creativity  — The ability to produce never-before-said sentences shows human creativity.
